System Architecture

Dual-mode range-extended EV (REEV)
⚡
Primary Drive: Electric
72V AC induction motor replaces the 1.5L dCi diesel engine. Rear drivetrain is removed; front-wheel drive retained via existing transmission/differential.
72V System 30kW Peak
⛽
Retained: Fuel Tank + Generator
Existing 50L fuel tank is retained. A 3–5kVA diesel generator is installed in the boot space to charge the battery when stationary — extending effective range.
50L Tank Kept Stationary Only
🔌
Dual Charging Inputs
Mode 1: AC grid charging via onboard charger (OBC) at 3.3kW. Mode 2: Diesel generator charges via same OBC interface. Both are stationary-only modes.

Step-by-Step Build Process

18 conversion phases — estimated 4–8 weeks
01
Documentation & Pre-Build Planning
Before touching the car, document everything. Photograph all wiring, engine bay, underside, and fuel system. Obtain original Verito workshop manual. Plan battery pack layout.
Verito Workshop Manual Battery Layout Drawing Component Sourcing List
💡 Measure available space under the floor, in the engine bay (for motor/controller), and in the boot (for generator). The Verito has ~800mm usable floor depth between front and rear axles.
02
Engine & Drivetrain Removal
Remove the 1.5L dCi diesel engine, fuel injection system, intercooler, exhaust system, and radiator. RETAIN: gearbox, driveshafts, hubs, brakes, steering, AC compressor connections.
Remove: Engine Remove: Exhaust Remove: Diesel Injectors Keep: Gearbox Keep: Driveshafts Keep: Fuel Tank
⚠️ Drain all fuel before engine removal. Dispose of old engine oil and diesel properly. Disconnect battery before any electrical work.
03
Motor Adapter Plate Fabrication
Custom machine a steel adapter plate to mate your chosen EV motor to the Verito's existing gearbox bell housing. The Verito uses a Renault/Dacia DP0 or JH3 gearbox depending on variant.
Bell Housing Pattern: Renault JH3 Steel: 15mm thickness Motor centering boss required
💡 Send accurate measurements of both motor end-face and gearbox bell-housing to a CNC machinist. Include motor shaft centerline and bolt circle dimensions. Cost: ₹12,000–22,000 for machining.
04
Motor & Gearbox Assembly
Mount the AC induction motor to the adapter plate. Install the custom shaft coupler between motor shaft and gearbox input. Torque all fasteners to spec. Test free rotation by hand.
Motor shaft alignment critical Coupler: keyed or splined Check for vibration clearance
⚠️ Motor and gearbox must be perfectly aligned. Misalignment causes vibration, bearing wear, and coupler failure. Use dial indicator for alignment check. Max 0.05mm runout.
05
Engine Bay Motor Installation
Lower the motor+gearbox assembly into the engine bay. Fabricate motor mounting brackets welded to existing engine mount points. The Verito engine bay comfortably fits medium-sized EV motors.
Reuse existing gearbox mounts Fabricate 2x front motor mounts Clear coolant hose paths for AC
💡 Mount motor controller, DC-DC, and OBC on a bracket panel on the firewall side or in place of the original radiator/intercooler space — good airflow available here.
06
Battery Box Fabrication & Mounting
Fabricate two aluminium/CRCA steel battery enclosures. Box 1 (main pack): fits under floor between axles. Box 2 (auxiliary cells if needed): in firewall or behind rear seat. Weld to floor sills.
IP65 sealed boxes Venting/pressure relief Ground clearance: min 150mm Insulate from chassis
⚠️ Battery boxes must be electrically isolated from the car chassis (floating ground system). Use rubber mounts. Add physical crash protection bars on exposed sides.
07
Cell Assembly & Battery Pack Build
Arrange LiFePO4 prismatic cells in 24S8P configuration (24 cells in series = 76.8V nominal, 8 parallel strings for 200Ah). Bus bars connect cells. Compress pack with end plates at ~10 PSI.
24S8P = 192 cells total Bus bars: 30mm × 3mm copper Cell compression: 10–15 PSI Balance cells before assembly
⚠️ Never short-circuit cells. Use insulated tools. Work in pairs. Install bus bars one row at a time. Cell voltage must be within 0.05V of each other before assembly.
💡 Order cells pre-matched (same batch, same capacity tested). Grade A cells from CALB/EVE cost more but are essential for longevity and safety.
08
BMS Installation & Configuration
Install Smart BMS (DALY/JK) on top of cell pack. Connect balance leads to each cell group (24 wires for 24S). Connect main power paths through BMS. Configure protection thresholds via app/software.
Overvoltage: 3.65V/cell Undervoltage: 2.80V/cell Over-temp: 55°C cutoff Balancing: 3.40V start
💡 Use JK BMS or ANT BMS for better app monitoring on Android. Configure all thresholds conservatively first — you can relax them after testing.
09
High-Voltage Wiring — Battery to Power Electronics
Run 35–70mm² orange HV cables from battery pack positive/negative through main fuse and contactor to motor controller. Install pre-charge resistor circuit (2-step inrush control).
Use 70mm² cable for main run Orange outer sheath = HV standard 400A ANL fuse at battery + Pre-charge: 100Ω / 50W resistor
⚠️ All HV work requires isolation. Use HV insulated gloves (Class 0, 1000V rated). Run HV cables away from chassis, secured in conduit. Never run HV and LV wires in same bundle.
10
Motor Controller Wiring & Configuration
Connect motor phase wires (3 × 35mm² to motor terminals U/V/W). Wire throttle pedal sensor (0–5V), brake sensor, forward/reverse switch, and key switch to controller. Configure via USB/CAN.
Kelly/SEVCON config software Motor phase calibration Throttle: 0.8V–4.2V range Regen braking level setup
💡 Set max current limits conservatively (60% of rated) for initial testing. Increase gradually after verifying thermal performance and motor response.
11
Onboard Charger (OBC) Installation
Mount OBC in engine bay on firewall bracket. Wire AC input (2.5mm² 3-pin with PE ground) to changeover switch. Wire DC output (16mm² to battery positive/negative via BMS). Install EVSE pilot circuit if required.
AC input: L+N+PE wired to switch DC output: to battery via BMS RCCB 30mA at AC input CAN communication to BMS
⚠️ OBC AC input must be properly earthed. Install RCCB (30mA) and MCB (16A) at the charging inlet. Never bypass the earth/PE connection.
12
Changeover Switch & Generator Connection
Install manual 32A changeover switch (weather resistant, in accessible location). Connect AC Mains cable to position 1 and Diesel Generator cable to position 2. Output goes to OBC AC input.
SPDT 32A 230V changeover Safety: never switch under load Weatherproof IP55 enclosure
⚠️ The changeover switch must be mechanically interlocked so both sources CANNOT be connected simultaneously. This prevents generator feedback into the mains grid (dangerous/illegal).
13
Diesel Generator — Boot/Underbody Installation
Install generator in boot space on vibration-isolating rubber mounts, OR under the car on a custom steel cradle. Run fuel line from retained 50L tank. Add exhaust pipe routed to vehicle side/rear.
Fuel line: 6mm armoured diesel hose Exhaust: stainless, side-exit CO sensor in boot area Vibration mounts: anti-shock rubber
⚠️ CRITICAL: Generator exhaust produces CO. Never run with boot closed. Consider under-car mounting with full-length exhaust exit at rear bumper. Install CO detector inside cabin as mandatory safety item.
14
DC-DC Converter & 12V System
Mount DC-DC converter in engine bay. Input: 72V from main pack. Output: 13.8V to 12V bus. This replaces the alternator function. Retain original 12V battery for initial startup reserve and emergency.
Charges 12V battery Powers: lights, ECU, wipers Powers: AC compressor relay Powers: power steering (if electric)
💡 If original Verito has hydraulic power steering with engine-driven pump — either replace with electric power steering motor, or source the EPS column from a Verito EV variant.
15
Charge Port Socket Installation
Cut and reinforce opening in the C-pillar (or original fuel flap location) for Type 2 charge inlet socket. Waterproof the cavity. Run cable to OBC input. Install pilot signal resistor for EVSE compatibility.
Type 2 IEC 62196 socket Locking actuator (optional) Pilot resistor: 2.74kΩ IP66 waterproof cavity
16
Dashboard & Instrumentation
Install EV instrument cluster showing: State of Charge (SOC), pack voltage, current draw, estimated range, temperature, regen level. Replace or supplement original analog cluster.
SOC % display Pack voltage + current Est. range km Regen level indicator Temp warnings
💡 JuncTek Bluetooth BMS monitor displays all data on phone. For in-dash display, a Raspberry Pi touchscreen with VictronConnect-style display works well and is fully custom.
17
Bench Tests & Static Power-On Sequence
Before driving: test each system individually. (1) Charge one cell group. (2) BMS communication test. (3) DC-DC output check. (4) OBC charge test. (5) Motor controller + motor spin test (wheels off ground).
Test sequence: BMS → OBC → Motor Check for HV faults/errors Log data via CAN on laptop
⚠️ First power-on of the HV system: wear HV gloves, have a second person present, have fire extinguisher ready. Connect battery through pre-charge circuit first.
18
Road Testing & Fine-Tuning
Begin with slow-speed trials in private area. Gradually increase speed and load. Test all functions: regen braking, charging modes, generator charging, range estimation accuracy. Adjust controller settings.
Week 1: parking lot tests Week 2: road 30–60km/h Week 3: highway 80–100km/h Test generator charging mode
💡 Keep a log of pack temperatures, SOC consumption per km, and regen effectiveness during testing. Typical: 10–12 Wh/km in city = ~130km from 14.4kWh pack.

Safety Requirements

Critical before, during, and after conversion
⚡
High Voltage Isolation
All HV wiring (72V+) must be in orange conduit or orange cable. Install manual service disconnect (MSD) in accessible location. Use isolation monitoring device (IMD) to detect ground faults. Minimum 500V insulation resistance between HV and chassis.
🧯
Fire Protection
LiFePO4 batteries are significantly safer than NMC but still require precautions. Install battery-area temperature sensors with automatic BMS cutoff at 55°C. Keep Class D or CO₂ fire extinguisher (2kg) in car. Never charge unattended for first 10 cycles.
💨
Carbon Monoxide — Generator Safety (CRITICAL)
Diesel generator produces CO. NEVER run with boot closed or in enclosed space. Install CO detector alarm inside cabin (mandatory). Design exhaust to exit at rear bumper, away from passenger compartment. Add generator run interlock that requires windows open / boot ajar.
🔌
Charging Safety Interlocks
Physical interlock: car cannot drive when charge cable plugged in. Changeover switch cannot connect both grid AND generator simultaneously. RCCB (30mA) on AC charging inlet. BMS cuts charging on cell overvoltage, over-temperature, or current overload.
🏛️
RTO Homologation & Legal Requirements (India)
EV conversions in India require RTO approval under AIS-123 guidelines (2021). The converted vehicle must pass a test by ARAI or iCAT. You'll need: NOC from manufacturer (Mahindra), conversion agency certification, revised RC book. Budget ₹15,000–40,000 for this process and approvals.
🛡️
Personal Protective Equipment
When working on HV systems: Class 0 HV insulating gloves (rated 1000V), safety glasses, insulating mat to stand on, rubber-soled footwear. Never work alone on HV. Use calibrated multimeter with HV-rated probes. Always disconnect HV and wait 5 minutes for capacitor discharge before touching HV components.
