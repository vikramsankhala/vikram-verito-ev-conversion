Technical Considerations

Why K9K is the Right Choice

  • The Mahindra Verito is a rebadged Renault Logan — it originally uses the K9K engine family, making this a like-for-like architectural swap.
  • The K9K 1.5 dCi 85 PS is the most widely available variant in Kurla donor markets.
  • Engine mounts are likely reusable or require minimal adaptation for Duster/Logan donors.
  • Common rail fuel system and ECU mapping are compatible with existing wiring harness.
  • Gearbox remains unchanged — only engine and mounting components require replacement.

Mandatory Work at Swap Time

  • Timing belt & tensioner must be replaced fresh — this is non-negotiable regardless of donor mileage.
  • Engine oil & filter (5W-30, 4 litres) must be fresh-filled before first start.
  • Coolant flush and hose inspection required; replace any degraded hoses.
  • Gasket and seal set to be fitted during engine installation.
  • ECU / sensor reconnection should be followed by diagnostic scan to clear codes.
  • Test drive and road tuning to confirm normal operation before handover.

Sourcing Criteria — Kurla Stall

  • Request a dealer-guaranteed, tested unit with documented mileage if possible.
  • Insist on a minimum 30-day parts warranty from the Kurla dealer stall.
  • Verify the unit visually — check for no cracks, rust, or oil sludge before purchase.
  • Ask for the donor car registration number for traceability.
  • Prefer Duster Gen 1 units; Logan units are equally acceptable.

What Does NOT Need Replacement

  • Gearbox / transmission — remains in place, reconnected after swap.
  • Body panels, suspension, brakes — reported in excellent condition.
  • Electrical system / wiring harness — compatible, no replacement needed.
  • Fuel tank, fuel lines — no change required.
  • Exhaust system — inspect flanges; likely reusable.
  • AC compressor — transfer from existing engine if serviceable.

Important Notes & Risk Flags

Price Variability Factors
  • Donor engine mileage and condition can shift engine cost by ₹3,000–₹8,000.
  • If engine mounts show wear and need replacement, add ₹1,500–₹3,000.
  • Discovery of additional worn components (water pump, thermostat) during disassembly may add ₹1,500–₹3,000.
  • Labour rates vary significantly between Navi Mumbai workshops — get 2–3 quotes.
  • Duster Gen 1 units command a slight premium over Logan at Kurla; both are acceptable.
What to Insist Upon
  • Itemised receipt for the donor engine with dealer's name and contact.
  • Used timing belt (old) returned to client as proof of replacement.
  • OBD diagnostic printout at handover showing zero active fault codes.
  • Test drive in client's presence before final payment.
  • Written warranty from workshop (even if informal).
Technical Caution Points
  • Do not accept a swap where the timing belt is not replaced fresh — this is the single highest risk item on a K9K engine.
  • Ensure the engine number is recorded and communicated to your insurance company post-swap (may affect policy).
  • Run engine at idle for 10+ minutes before test drive — monitor temperature and oil pressure carefully.
  • If ECU errors persist after reconnection, a K9K-specialist diagnostic session may be needed.
Summary — Client's Position

Vikram Sankhala's request is technically straightforward — the Mahindra Verito is architecturally a Renault Logan, and the K9K engine is the factory-fitted unit. This is a same-family engine swap, not a conversion. The budget estimate of ₹61,200–₹78,700 is fair and realistic for the Mumbai market as of 2026, assuming a dealer-grade Kurla stall unit and a competent local diesel workshop. No unusual risk factors are present.
